Overview

Burger Ops is an educational card game my group designed to teach service design methods in a playful, hands-on way. Set in a fast-food franchise during a supply chain crisis, players act as managers racing to assemble their signature burgers while dealing with real-world service design challenges. The game combines a competitive Phase 1 with a collaborative Phase 2, where players ultimately work together to keep the franchise ‚Äúalive,‚Äù reinforcing both individual problem-solving and teamwork.

Design rationale

Visually, we used the burger as the core metaphor to make abstract service design concepts more concrete and approachable. Each card type‚Äîingredients, obstacles, shipments, orders‚Äîhas its own layout and visual hierarchy so players can quickly recognize its function during the game. Colours, icons, and typography are chosen to feel like a fast-food brand (warm, bold, friendly) while still being clean enough to support reading, debating, and checking methods from the book. The ingredient stacks and burger layers give a clear sense of progress, helping players literally ‚Äúsee‚Äù their learning and strategy building up over time.

Design process

We started by defining the learning objectives‚Äîwhat service design methods to cover and how players would practise them through discussion and decision-making. Then we built a competitive-to-collaborative structure, card types, and win conditions around those goals. After testing the rules, facilitator notes, and quick reference guide, we refined the wording, layout, and information hierarchy so the flow stayed clear and never felt overwhelming.
